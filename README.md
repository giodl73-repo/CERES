# CERES — Local Production Atlas

Can a town of five thousand restore a working forge, a bakery that sells
wholesale, a weaving co-op? CERES tests whether the answer is yes — and
why, for whom, and at what cost.

---

## What This Is

CERES is a catalog-driven design and evaluation project for modern
artisan-production equipment: forges, ovens, looms, kilns, and related
trades. The catalog anchors each design in historical functional
requirements — what a forge actually had to do across medieval Northern
Europe, Song-era China, Tokugawa Japan — then evaluates every design
against three economic lenses (market / cooperative / civic) at three
settlement scales (village 500–2,000; town 2,000–15,000; small city
15,000–75,000). The organizing question is whether meaningful local
production can be restored, not assumed. The working hypothesis is
testable and has registered falsifiers before Phase 1 evidence arrives.
A rigorous null result — showing that equipment is not the binding
constraint — is as valid as a positive finding.

## Named After

**CeCe** — Giovanni's chicken, alive and well.
**Ceres** — Roman goddess of grain, harvest, and the nourishment of
settlements. Not alive, but still relevant.

The naming pattern runs across sibling projects: **LUCIA** takes its
name from Lucy, Giovanni's German Shorthaired Pointer; **FELICE** from
Felix, another of Giovanni's pets. CERES continues the pattern.
