# P-6 — The Skeptical Funder — Review of ceres-design (R1)

**Overall:** The spec is more disciplined than most proposals I see — explicit non-goals, named vertical slice, layered simulation with earned escalation, three economic lenses rather than triumphal market-only framing. But it will not survive my read. It never defines what *success* looks like for the project itself, it has no timeline, it has no audience for the pitch narrative, and it has no answer to the single question that determines whether I cut the check: what does the world look like if CERES delivers everything promised, and is that outcome worth the capital and years it took to produce?

**What works:**
- Section 3 locks scope decisions as a table. I can see what was decided without reading five pages of prose.
- Section 10 "definition of done" for the vertical slice is concrete and verifiable: specific files, specific counts, specific schema-completion bar. That is how you scope a first pilot.
- Section 12 non-goals is honest about what this is not. Most proposals I read hide their non-goals behind optimistic silence; this one states them. Good.
- The layered-simulation approach (Tier A comprehensive, Tier B conditional, Tier C selective) shows discipline about cost vs. value of each fidelity level. This is the same logic I apply when deciding how much diligence to run on a deal.
- The name, the narrative framing, and the sibling-project context establish credibility quickly. You understand how to open a pitch.

**What doesn't:**
- **No success criterion for the project as a whole.** Section 10 defines "done for the vertical slice" in terms of artifacts produced. It does not define whether the produced artifacts constitute a successful project or a failed one. If all 15 forge entries return "fail" across all 9 cells, is that a scientific null result (success — we learned local smithing cannot be restored by equipment redesign alone) or a project failure (we did not find any winning design)? You must state this before starting, not after.
- **No timeline.** How long is the vertical slice expected to take — one quarter, two quarters, a year? Funders commit capital against time. A project without a timeline is a project without a burn rate.
- **No defined audience for the pitch narrative.** "Fundable" was specified, but the funder archetype was not. Single philanthropist? Civic foundation? Municipal economic-development grant? Family office? These are different pitches, different asks, different quantitative bars. The spec defers this.
- **No intermediate gate between research and catalog.** A reader following the spec could begin catalog authorship before the research corpus has converged on functional requirements — producing entries grounded in assumptions rather than evidence. An explicit Phase 1 → Phase 2 gate is missing.
- **The central claim is untested.** Section 2 argues that the equipment, not the demand, is the constraint. If the research reveals that demand is actually the constraint (consumer preference for industrial goods is durable), the entire project is aimed at the wrong lever. There is no pre-registered falsifier for this.
- **What does the successful world look like?** The spec describes the project's artifacts. It does not describe the change in the world those artifacts cause. In a fundable plan, the last page is the difference between "we built a rigorous catalog" and "three towns piloted catalog-derived designs and two of them worked." The spec points at the first; a funder acts on the second.

**The question I'd push on:**
Before I write a check, give me three answers in under 200 words each: (1) If the vertical slice completes and every catalog entry fails viability across all 9 cells, is CERES a successful scientific null result or a failed project — and which are you pre-registering? (2) Who is the specific funder archetype the pitch narrative targets, and what dollar order-of-magnitude is the ask? (3) What is the world-level outcome you are accountable for, beyond the artifacts the spec lists? No project that cannot answer these three questions is fundable, however rigorous the rest of the work.
