# Baking — Consolidated Bibliography and Placeholder Audit

## Purpose

This document consolidates every citation across the seven Plan F deliverables for the
baking trade (Tasks 1–7). It serves two functions. First, it is the bibliography: all
named sources grouped by subject area, with full citations, one-line usage notes, and
confidence tiers. Second, it is the author's verification queue: every
`[CITATION-NEEDED: ...]` placeholder across the seven documents is listed in the
Placeholder Audit table, with a suggested research lead and urgency ranking. Items
blocking Plan G catalog consumption are flagged explicitly, with emphasis on the
mill-dependency falsifier — the single most important cross-cultural finding — and
the four specific gaps that prevent confident catalog-entry authorship. No new
citations are introduced here.

seven Plan F documents is listed here. Rows are sorted by urgency: items blocking Plan G
catalog authorship appear first; items needed for polish only appear last.

**Deduplication methodology:** Placeholder topics are deduplicated across all occurrences.
A single research gap that appears multiple times inline across documents and/or in source
lists is counted once, with all document locations listed in the Location(s) column. The
four cultural chapters and three synthesis documents together contained approximately 150
inline placeholder markers; these have been collapsed to 63 distinct citation gaps by
merging multiple occurrences of the same underlying research question. The mill-dependency
falsifier is the single most critical finding for Plan G; placeholders bearing on it are
marked P1-BLOCKING regardless of which document they appear in.

**Urgency key:**
- **P1-BLOCKING** — Plan G catalog entries will misstate facts or carry unacceptable
  uncertainty if this remains unresolved; catalog consumption cannot begin for the
  affected sub-claim
- **P2-LOAD-BEARING** — supports a specific factual claim frequently cited downstream;
  should be verified before Plan G authorship begins
- **P3-SUPPORTING** — adds precision or corroboration; main claim survives without it
- **P4-POLISH** — useful for completeness; does not affect any synthesis claim

## Recommended Verification Priority

The following items should be resolved before Plan G catalog authorship begins. Each is
listed with the reason it is load-bearing for catalog consumption. Items 1–5 are
P1-BLOCKING: catalog entries **cannot** be considered complete until these are resolved.
Items 6–10 are P2-LOAD-BEARING and are critical path for individual sub-claims that
appear across multiple catalog entries.

**The mill-dependency falsifier and Plan G.** The single most important cross-cultural
finding — that no baking operation in any culture milled its own grain at commercial
scale, and that the mill is the binding upstream constraint — is well-sourced from
Langdon 2004, Jordan 1996, and Cronon 1991. This finding does NOT appear in the P1
list because the falsifier itself is sourced. What is P1-BLOCKING is the downstream
question: what does commercial flour actually cost at the volume relevant to a small
artisan bakery, and what does leavening cost? Those gaps (Placeholders #1 and #4)
prevent Plan G from building economically grounded catalog entries even though the
historical record is clear. Plan G must address flour cost and leavening cost from
current sources, not from the historical record alone.
